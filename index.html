Children of immigrants account for nearly one-quarter of all children in the United States. Although the growth in the number of children with at least one immigrant parent began to slow down in 2011, the share of children of immigrants continues to grow. In fact, because the number of children born to two native parents has dropped, the increase in the number of children of immigrants is responsible for all population growth among US children from 2006 to 2013.

As the share of children born to immigrant parents continues to grow, up-to-date information about children of immigrants and the social policies that affect them is essential. Understanding the characteristics of children of immigrants as well as the trends at the state and metro area is vital in considering policies that affect children and family.
